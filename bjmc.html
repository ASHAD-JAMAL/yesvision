BJMC Course Details

  • BJMC full form is Bachelor of Journalism and Mass Communication.
  • The BJMC course duration is for a period of 3 years.
  • The BJMC course is available in full-time (Regular mode) as well as in distance mode.
  • The entire BJMC syllabus is divided into 6 semesters spread across the 3 years duration. The BJMC subjects include CyberMedia, Information Society, Public Relations Theory, Media Law and Ethics, etc.
  • BJMC full form Bachelor of Journalism and Mass Communication is a course that can be pursued by candidates who have completed their 12th in any stream with a minimum score of 50%. A 5% relaxation is provided to the candidates belonging to reserved categories.
  • The BJMC admissions are done either on the basis of merit or entrance examination.
  • Entrance examinations such as IPU CET, IIMC, DUET etc are conducted by universities and Institutes to carry out admissions in BJMC course.
  • The top BJMC colleges offering BJMC courses are Guru Gobind Singh Indraprastha University, Mewar University, Indira Gandhi National Tribal University, National Institute of Mass Communication and Journalism, etc.
  • The average cost of the course ranges between INR 50,000-INR 300,000
  • Working professionals who wish to pursue a career in journalism and mass communication can opt for the Distance BJMC course.
  • Candidates can pursue BJMC course or similar courses abroad. However, the tuition fees are comparatively higher than in India. The average tuition fees range between INR 13,00,000 to INR 24,00,000. In some cases, it can move up to 40 lakhs and beyond.
  • Candidates after completion of the BJMC course are hired in the different job roles of the media and entertainment industry.
  • According to a report by the Times of India, the average BJMC salary ranges between INR 300,000-INR 400,000.
  • The top BJMC job profiles are News Analyst, Radio Jockey, Photojournalist, Journalist,and many more

What is BJMC Course?

BJMC or Bachelor of Journalism & Mass Communication teaches the skills and knowledge of journalism like collecting and delivering information. The BJMC course teaches skills in software, communication, equipment, writing and analytical ability in its three-year undergraduate course.

Who should do BJMC Course?

  • The BJMC course should be pursued by the candidates who are interested in pursuing a career in journalism, creative writing, publication, or mass communication.
  • A career in journalism or mass communication requires the candidates to have excellent skills in communication, presentation, creative writing, presentation. Thus, the candidates with the following skills should take up the following course.
  • Individuals who want to inspire people against social injustice or inequalities with their writing skills and thought should pursue a course in BJMC.
  • Similarly, candidates who want to pursue a career in copywriting can take up the BJMC course. Similarly, candidates with an interest in editorial works can also take up the BJMC course.
  • The candidates who want to pursue a job that involves a lot of traveling should take up the BJMC course.
  • A career as a journalist and media person can lead to various challenging scenarios almost every day. Candidates who are willing to take risks should only go for the BJMC course.

    • BJMC Eligibility

    The eligibility criteria for the BJMC admission varies slightly from one college to another, however, the basic eligibility criteria remain the same for all the institutions:

    • The candidates are required to score a minimum aggregate 50% score in 10+2 from a recognized board or university. In the case of admission for the reserved categories the candidates are offered a relaxation of 5%.
    • The age of the aspirant should be 17 years or above.
    • The candidates appearing for the final examination are also eligible to apply for the courses.
