What is B Pharma Course?

Bachelor of Pharmacy, as the name suggests, has a course structure that deals with the production and manufacturing of drugs and their distributions with a particular specialization in pharmaceutical studies.
A few of the most vital subjects included in the B Pharm course study are Biochemistry, Human Anatomy and Physiology, Computer Applications in Pharmacy etc

B Pharmacy is 4 year duration undergraduate degree course designed to study the manufacturing process of medicines helping students understand the process of dispensing medicines across medical stores and distributors. Students need to have atleast 45% in PCM subjects in class 12 in order to meet B Pharma Eligibility.

B Pharma Fees range from INR 1,25,000 to INR 3,50,000. B Pharmacy course Fees are highest in private colleges like NMIMS, BIT Mesra, etc

Why study B Pharma Course?

There are several interesting points that can be jotted down, as to why you should pursue B Pharmacy. B Pharmacy Course Fees is very much within the limits of the majority of the students.
Apart from the fact that B Pharmacy course will help you in making a career as a Medical Representative, Scientific Writing, Medical Scripting, Clinical Research etc we will discuss in detail other reasons for pursuing this course.

  • Government Jobs : After successfully completing your degree in B Pharmacy you can get the opportunity to work for various Government organizations and hospitals.
  • Work with Patients: Apart from being a pharmacist you will have crucial roles in the health care system such as providing asthma care, cholesterol control checking, diabetes disease management, bone density scans etc.
  • High FDI in the sector: Between April-December 2021, FDI inflows reached USD 1.2 Billion in the drugs and pharmaceutical sectors. The drugs and pharmaceuticals sector of India received cumulative FDIs worth USD 19.41 Billion between April 2000-March 2022.
  • High Industry Growth: India has a distinct advantage in the production and distribution of various kinds of drugs. India exported USD 5.7 Billion worth of drugs between April 2021- June 2021. The industry supplies over 50% of the global demand for vaccines, 40% of generic drug demand in the US and 25% of all medicine in the UK.
  • Diverse Career Opportunity: India is the largest producer of generic drugs globally. The most obvious choice for pharmacists is working in a retail pharmacy, either in a health care facility or community. The role involves giving over the counter and prescription medications along with counseling to the patients.
  • Stable yet Flexible Career: A bachelor's degree in pharmacy will not only provide stable employment throughout your career but also will give you the flexibility to choose and work according to your preferences.

Who Should do a B Pharmacy

  • Students who wish to make a career in the healthcare sector must pursue B.Pharmacy.
  • Candidates who wish to pursue a career in the Pharmaceutics Industry and want to start with a diploma course, can seek admission to the B Pharma course.
  • Candidates who wish to pursue MBA in Pharmaceutical Management can also pursue a B Pharmacy course.
